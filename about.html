Hey! My name is Liene and I love to travel. I have always felt the calling to travel North and meet the magnificent mountains painted by the magical Aurora borealis on starry winter nights. I also like learning about other cultures and exploring new places. I have spent my ERASMUS studies in the waterfall-rich Iceland, where I fell in love with the north. Luckily, I was blessed with a pos-graduate ERASMUS internship, which gave me a chance to live abroad once more. I had been in Denmark and my husband had lived in Finland. Sweden was a country no-one I knew had ever been. Naturally, that's where I went.

My Swedish story starts on the 15th of August, when I got accepted as a communication intern at The Department of Environment, Ecology and Plant Science (DEEP) of Stockholm University. After that there was a lot of work to do: getting all the documents, finding a place to live, buying tickets, planning the arrival etc. Finding a place to live in Stockholm was and adventure in itself. I had been following a YouTube channel created by a swede living there. Lucky for me, he had a video just about that. Most of the sites he mentioned where in Swedish. However, with the reasonably good help of Google Translate, I managed to get the general idea.

An ERASMUS internship is an amazing adventure - you get to live in another country and get a scholarship for it. Because of it, it is extremely important and necessary to share ones experience with everyone else, especially, potential student travellers. This website was born out of a wish to tell others about my experience and share some of the most colorful impressions.

Sweden is part of the geographical area of Fennoscandia. The climate is in general mild for its northerly latitude due to significant maritime influence. In spite of the high latitude, Sweden often has warm continental summers, being located in between the North Atlantic, the Baltic Sea and the vast Eurasian Russian landmass. The general climate and environment vary significantly from the south and north due to the vast latitudal difference, and much of Sweden has reliably cold and snowy winters. Southern Sweden is predominantly agricultural, while the north is heavily forested and includes a portion of the Scandinavian Mountains.
